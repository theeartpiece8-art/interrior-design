Two commitments sit at the centre of our studio practice — building our own custom paint colours by hand, and training the next generation of East African designers and makers.

The Mix Lab

We mix our own paints, in our own colours.

Most of the region's available paint colours are developed for climates and light very different from ours. So in 2022 we started the Mix Lab — an in-studio programme where we blend pigments by hand to build a custom colour library suited to East African light, clay tones and interiors.

Every colour in the Mix Lab library is tested on-site across multiple rooms and times of day before it's added to a project palette. Several of our signature tones — including our terracotta-clay and papyrus-green bases — are now available directly through our Shop.

How It Works

From pigment to palette

  • Source

    Natural and mineral pigments are sourced from regional suppliers wherever possible.

  • Mix

    Our in-house colourist blends small batches by hand, testing against local light conditions.

  • Test

    Swatches are trialled on real walls across different rooms and times of day before approval.

  • Apply

    Approved colours join our library and are used across client projects and our Shop.

Upskilling & Internships

Training the next generation of East African designers.

Good design talent shouldn't have to leave the region to find opportunity. Since 2021, our studio has run a standing internship programme for design, architecture and horticulture graduates and students across Uganda, Kenya and Rwanda.

Interns are paired one-to-one with senior designers, rotate through interiors, gardens and the Mix Lab, and leave with a portfolio of real client work. Several of our current studio leads — including our Head of Interiors — started as Mix Lab interns.
