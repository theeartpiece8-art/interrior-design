Our landscape practice sits alongside our interiors work — gardens, courtyards and outdoor living spaces designed around native planting, water efficiency and long-term maintenance.

Our Commitment

Sustainability isn't a feature. It's the starting brief.

Every garden we design begins with a site and water assessment, not a plant list. We favour species native to the Lake Victoria basin and East African highlands, reduce irrigation dependency through swale and rain-garden systems, and specify reclaimed stone and timber wherever we can source it.

We also work hard to design gardens that stay beautiful with modest maintenance — because a sustainable garden is one a household or hotel can actually keep alive.

01

Native Planting

We prioritise indigenous and naturalised species suited to local rainfall, reducing water use and supporting pollinators.

02

Water-Wise Design

Rain gardens, swales and greywater-ready irrigation are considered from the first site visit, not added later.

03

Reclaimed Materials

Salvaged stone, timber and clay pavers are sourced before new materials are specified.

04

Biodiversity

Every garden brief includes a habitat goal — for birds, pollinators or beneficial insects.

Our Approach

How a garden comes together

  • Site Assessment

    Soil, water flow, existing planting and microclimate are mapped before any design begins.

  • Design

    A planting and hardscape plan built around native species and reclaimed materials.

  • Planting

    Installation phased by season, working with regional nurseries and our own growers.

  • Maintenance

    A light-touch maintenance plan handed to the household or hotel, built for the long term.
